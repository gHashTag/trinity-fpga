## Why Ternary?

### Information Density

<div class="formula">

Binary: **log₂(2) = 1.00 bits/digit**

Ternary: **log₂(3) = 1.58 bits/digit**

</div>

Ternary achieves **58.5% more information** per digit.

### Optimal Radix

The radix that minimizes `r × ⌈log_r(N)⌉` for storing N distinct values is `e ≈ 2.718`.

Since radix must be integer: **3 is the optimal choice**.

This maps naturally to:
- Spin states in quantum mechanics
- Signal directions in electronics
- Force vectors in physics
