# Hall of Fame: Mathematicians Behind Trinity

Trinity stands on the shoulders of giants. This page honors the mathematicians, physicists, and computer scientists whose discoveries form the foundation of ternary computing, hyperdimensional computing, and the mathematical framework that unifies them.

---

## I. Antiquity — The Foundations

| Mathematician | Era | Discovery | Role in Trinity |
|---------------|-----|-----------|------------------|
| **Euclid of Alexandria** | ~300 BCE | Golden ratio (Elements, Book VI, Definition 3: "extreme and mean ratio") | Theorem 1 (φ² + 1/φ² = 3), all phi properties |
| **Archimedes of Syracuse** | ~250 BCE | π approximations (223/71 < π < 22/7), measurement of the circle | Constant π in sacred formulas |
| **Claudius Ptolemy** | 150 CE | Almagest, geocentric model | Astronomical constants |
| **Diophantus of Alexandria** | ~250 CE | Arithmetica (Diophantine equations) | Integer algebra, number theory |
