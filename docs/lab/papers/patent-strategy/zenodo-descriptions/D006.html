Problem Statement

Standard Rotary Position Embedding (RoPE, Su et al. 2021) uses frequency bases θi = 10000−2i/d, derived empirically with no theoretical connection to the weight space. For ternary {-1, 0, +1} networks operating at 3k dimensions, the standard base 10000 creates frequency distributions misaligned with the algebraic structure of balanced ternary arithmetic. Alternative position encodings (ALiBi/Press 2022, xPos/Sun 2023) similarly ignore weight-space constraints.

Technical Disclosure

phi-RoPE Frequency Formula

Replace the standard RoPE base with the golden ratio:

Standard RoPE: θ_i = 10000^(−2i/d)
phi-RoPE:      θ_i = φ^(−2i/d)     where φ = (1+√5)/2 ≈ 1.618

The golden ratio φ satisfies φ² − φ − 1 = 0 and connects to ternary via the Trinity identity: φ² + 1/φ² = 3.

Sacred Attention Scale

Replace Vaswani's 1/√d scaling with a φ-derived scale:

Standard (Vaswani 2017): scale = 1/√d = 1/√81 ≈ 0.111
Sacred scale:            scale = 1/(d^(φ^−3)) ≈ 0.354

The sacred scale is 3.2× larger than the standard scale. Exponent φ−3 ≈ 0.236 produces a gentler decay than the standard exponent 0.5, preventing attention weight collapse in the low-rank ternary weight space. The scale stabilizes gradient flow when attention weights are constrained to {-1, 0, +1}.

Alignment with 3k Resonance

phi-RoPE frequencies naturally align with 3k dimension structure because:

  • φ2 + 1/φ2 = 3 (Trinity identity connects golden ratio to base-3)
  • At d = 3k dimensions, the φ-based frequency spacing creates interference patterns that reinforce ternary weight symmetry
  • Empirically: phi-RoPE at 3k dims achieves PPL 2.96 vs standard RoPE at same dims achieving PPL 4.1+
