**Subject:** Question about φ-based formulas in physics — structure or numerology?

Dear Professor Baez,

I've been following your Azimuth blog posts distinguishing numerology from structural physics. I'm hoping you could help evaluate something I've encountered.

I'm building an open-source ternary computing framework (Trinity) grounded in φ² + φ⁻² = 3. The engineering results are concrete: $30 FPGA running a ternary LLM at 63 tok/s, ~1W, zero DSP blocks.

The mathematics led to φ-based expressions that approximate some physical constants:
- m_p/m_e ≈ 6π⁵ (0.002% error)
- Several relations for CKM/PMNS mixing angles
- Gravitational constant G from π³γ²/φ (0.09% error)

**But** — I've also documented predictions that failed. I maintain an "Evidence Ladder" grading each claim as Established, Speculative, or Rejected. For example:
- **Rejected**: γ = φ⁻³ for Barbero-Immirzi parameter (0.617% error, but wrong sign)
- **Rejected**: α family fine-structure constant relations
- **Established**: Ternary FPGA implementation (deployed, running)

I cannot tell if there's genuine mathematical structure here or if I'm pattern-matching on constants. Your work distinguishing "ugly coincidences" from "beautiful necessities" makes you one of the few who could evaluate this.

**Repository**: https://github.com/gHashTag/trinity
**Evidence Ladder**: https://github.com/gHashTag/trinity#evidence-ladder (README heading with Smoking Guns, Consistent, Rejected)
**Scientific overview**: https://github.com/gHashTag/trinity/blob/main/docs/papers/README_FOR_SCIENTISTS.md

If you have a moment to skim, I would value your assessment: pursue this direction or abandon as numerology?

With gratitude,

Dmitrii Vasilev
